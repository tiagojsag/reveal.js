Agile

What is (and isn't) agile?

Agile is not dailies, sprints, story points or backlogs

Those are practices to implement the principles

We can use them, or something else entirely

Agile is not a fixed rule set

Agile is about experimenting and seeing what works for you

We can have our own approach and still be agile

Agile is not only for developers

Many different types of teams use agile

Agile can help designers, data scientists, ....

Agile is a set of 12 principles

Agile defines how people approach their teams, their projects and stakeholders

Agile is for everyone in the team - including customers

Agile involves everyone in the project - including customers - in all stages

Some customers prefer or even require agile practices

Agile can promote involvement from different roles

Agile is adaptable

Agile comes with toolboxes (XP, Scrum, Kanban, ...)

Pick what works for you, discard the rest

If nothing else works, create new tools

Agile is flexible

We already use some of the agile guidelines

No need for disruptive changes - implement step by step

Consolidate before moving on

Agile principles

Agile is a set of principles

There are 12 principles

Let's talk about some of them

Agile Principles

"Our highest priority is to satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery of valuable software."

  • Deliver valuable features first
  • "Value" is not defined by us, but by the customer (with our help)
  • Deliver small, finished features

Agile Principles

"Business people and developers must work together daily throughout the project."

  • Communicate inside and outside the team
  • Learn from and teach your team mates
  • Talk with customers - it helps you and them

Agile Principles

"Build projects around motivated individuals. Give them the environment and support they need, and trust them to get the job done."

  • Rely on your team - they are there for you
  • If you need something, ask

Agile Principles

"Working software is the primary measure of progress."

  • Customers want software that adds value
  • All other metrics are secondary

Agile Principles

"Continuous attention to technical excellence and good design enhances agility."

  • No hacks, no haf-baked solutions
  • If something needs refactoring, that should happen ASAP

Agile Principles

"The best architectures, requirements, and designs emerge from self-organizing teams."

  • Decisions should be made as team - big and small
  • Everyone involved should participate

Agile Principles

"At regular intervals, the team reflects on how to become more effective, then tunes and adjusts its behavior accordingly."

  • Give, accept and promote feedback
  • Don't be afraid to be honest - if you give honest feedback, you will be sure you are getting honest feedback
